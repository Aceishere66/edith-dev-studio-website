# Visual Direction

## Desired Feel

EDITH Dev Studio should feel like a dark premium creative software studio: technical, high-control, design-conscious, and direct. The site should be memorable without looking noisy or theatrical.

## Palette

- Base: near-black and deep graphite.
- Primary energy: violet.
- Secondary energy: blue/cyan.
- Copy: high-contrast white and muted cool gray.
- Warnings/constraints: amber only where factual caution is needed.

Avoid beige, brown/orange, washed-out SaaS blue, or one-note purple surfaces.

## Composition

- Use full-width dark bands and restrained layered surfaces.
- Reserve the strongest visual weight for the homepage hero and Upscaler Studio product hero.
- Companion pages should feel coherent but lighter than the canonical product page.
- Avoid repeated identical card stacks; vary rhythm with feature rows, split panels, dense trust strips, and product frames.

## Current Visual Language

- CSS-generated product surfaces instead of random external assets.
- Layered radial gradients for depth.
- Thin borders and soft glows on important product panels.
- Tight, clear typography with no viewport-width font scaling.
- Responsive grids that collapse cleanly on mobile.

## Future Asset Direction

Needed real assets:

- Upscaler Studio app screenshot.
- Before/after image enhancement comparison.
- Product mockup or desktop frame.
- Optional future 3D/interactive product moment.

All assets must be owned, licensed for commercial use, or created internally.

## Motion Direction

Motion should come after the foundation is trustworthy. Use subtle CSS transitions first. If a later phase adds motion or 3D, keep it to one strong section and validate performance on mobile.
